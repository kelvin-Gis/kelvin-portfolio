GIS Research JUL 24, 2025

GIS-Based Site Selection for Telecommunication Infrastructure

Project Overview

This project focuses on applying GIS and Remote Sensing techniques to analyze spatial patterns and support decision-making in urban planning and infrastructure development.

Key Insights

  • Urban growth is strongly concentrated along major transport corridors.
  • Areas with poor accessibility show slower development rates.
  • Spatial data reveals clear clustering of high-demand zones for infrastructure.

Problem Identified

Lack of optimized spatial planning has led to uneven service distribution and inefficient infrastructure placement.

GIS Approach / Methodology

  • Data collection from satellite imagery and open spatial datasets
  • Spatial analysis using ArcGIS / QGIS
  • Hotspot and proximity analysis
  • Map visualization and interpretation

Tools Used

ArcGIS, QGIS, Excel, Remote Sensing Data, GPS Data

Outcome

The analysis helped identify optimal zones for infrastructure planning and improved understanding of spatial development trends.

GIS Applications in Fiber Network Planning

Key Insights

  • Fiber network planning is highly dependent on spatial demand patterns, with dense urban and commercial zones requiring higher bandwidth and infrastructure concentration.
  • Road network alignment is a critical factor in fiber routing, as it minimizes excavation costs and simplifies installation logistics.
  • GIS analysis reveals that areas near existing infrastructure are more cost-effective for expansion compared to isolated or poorly connected zones.
  • Physical constraints such as rivers, flood-prone areas, and built-up congestion significantly influence route optimization decisions.
  • Buffer analysis around existing fiber lines helps identify underserved areas and potential expansion corridors.
  • Central business districts often have redundant fiber coverage, while peri-urban areas show gaps in network resilience and backup connectivity.
  • Integrating GIS into fiber planning improves decision-making by balancing cost efficiency, coverage optimization, and service reliability.

Overall, GIS provides a strategic framework for optimizing fiber network design by integrating spatial demand, infrastructure constraints, and cost considerations.

Remote Sensing for Environmental Monitoring

Key Insights

  • Satellite imagery enables continuous monitoring of environmental changes across large geographic areas, providing timely and cost-effective observations.
  • Multi-temporal image analysis reveals trends in land cover change, helping identify areas experiencing rapid environmental transformation.
  • Vegetation indices such as NDVI are effective in detecting changes in vegetation health, highlighting regions affected by drought, degradation, or human activities.
  • Remote sensing data supports early detection of environmental issues, allowing stakeholders to respond before significant damage occurs.
  • Spatial analysis shows that urban expansion often contributes to the reduction of natural vegetation and increased pressure on surrounding ecosystems.
  • Integrating remote sensing with GIS improves the visualization and interpretation of environmental patterns, supporting evidence-based decision-making.
  • Long-term satellite observations provide valuable insights into climate-related impacts, resource management, and environmental sustainability.

Overall, remote sensing serves as a powerful tool for environmental monitoring by delivering accurate, up-to-date spatial information that supports conservation, planning, and sustainable resource management.

Identifying Pollution Hotspots Using GIS

Key Insights

  • Spatial analysis revealed that pollution incidents are not evenly distributed but tend to cluster in specific areas, forming identifiable pollution hotspots.
  • High concentrations of pollution were commonly associated with densely populated neighborhoods, industrial zones, and areas with inadequate waste management systems.
  • Hotspot analysis helped prioritize locations requiring immediate environmental intervention and monitoring efforts.
  • Proximity analysis indicated a strong relationship between pollution levels and nearby pollution sources such as waste disposal sites, industrial facilities, and major transportation corridors.
  • GIS visualization made it easier to communicate complex environmental patterns, supporting informed decision-making by planners and environmental authorities.
  • Areas identified as pollution hotspots often overlapped with vulnerable communities, highlighting potential public health and environmental justice concerns.
  • Integrating spatial data from multiple sources improved the accuracy of hotspot identification and provided a more comprehensive understanding of pollution distribution.

Overall, GIS proved to be an effective tool for identifying pollution hotspots, enabling data-driven environmental management, targeted mitigation strategies, and more efficient allocation of monitoring resources.
